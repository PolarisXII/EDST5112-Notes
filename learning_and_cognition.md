### Perspectives about learning 

#### Cognitive perspective 
An information processing system that is concerned with how the mind perceives, processes, stores and retrieves information. 

#### Behavioural perspective 
- A focus on externally observable behaviours as indicators of learning. 
- Modify the behaviours to reflect learning using tools such as rewards. 
- Using environment to influence behaviour as learning was seen as responses to the environment. 

### What is learned?

#### Biologically Primary Knowledge
- Skills we acquire naturally from the environment 
- No special motivation requirements 
- i.e: speaking and listening one's first knowledge, counting, basic logic, general problem solving

#### Biologically Secondary Knowledge
- Knowledge and skills we are not directly evolved to acquire.
- We decide to learn them because they are culturally significant. 
- i.e: reading and writing, advanced mathematics 

- **Domain-specific knowledge**: 
    - Biologically secondary knowledge usually fall under this
    - Highly organised 
    - Evidenced in differences between novices and experts 

### Principles of Information Processing Systems

**Information store** -> store information 
- Long term memory mostly domain-specfic knowledge 
- **Learning** is a change in information store

**Borrowing and reorganising** -> obtain information from others 
- Reorganisation enables **teaching**

**Randomeness as genesis** -> generate novel information 
- A general problem solving skill 
- Gives us creativity 

**Narrow limits of change** -> restrict randomness to protect information store 
**Environmental organising and linking** -> Use stored information to determine action in a given environment 
- Allows us to apply what we learned in unseen environments
